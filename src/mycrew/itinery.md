As your Daily Itinerary Planner, I have designed a comprehensive and balanced 7-day itinerary for your business-focused trip to Lagos from October 18th to October 25th, 2025.

This plan is tailored to your interests in Business, Tech & Innovation, Research, and Professional Development. It maximizes your professional engagements while allowing for cultural immersion, rest, and spontaneous discovery. The schedule is based on an arrival on Sunday, October 19th, and departure on Saturday, October 25th, assuming you are staying in the Victoria Island/Ikoyi area.

### **Lagos Business & Innovation Itinerary: October 19 - 25, 2025**

---

### **Day 1: Sunday, 19th October - Arrival & Acclimatization**

**Theme:** Seamless arrival and settling into the vibrant energy of Lagos.

*   **5:30 PM - 7:00 PM (1.5 hours): Arrival & Immigration**
    *   **Activity:** Arrive at Murtala Muhammed International Airport (LOS). Clear immigration and baggage claim. It is highly recommended to have arranged a pre-booked, reputable car service for airport pickup.
    *   **Tip:** The airport can be busy. A pre-arranged driver waiting for you will provide a secure and stress-free transfer to your accommodation.

*   **7:00 PM - 8:30 PM (1.5 hours): Transfer & Check-in**
    *   **Activity:** Transfer to your accommodation in Victoria Island or Ikoyi. Settle in and freshen up.
    *   **Note:** Evening traffic can be unpredictable; this time allows for potential delays.

*   **8:30 PM onwards: Welcome Dinner**
    *   **Dining Option:** **Glover Court Suya.** For a relaxed and authentic first taste of Lagos, ask your driver to stop at this famous spot for some of the best suya (spicy grilled meat skewers) in the city. Alternatively, have a quiet dinner at your accommodation or a nearby hotel restaurant.
    *   **Objective:** Ease into your trip with a low-key evening.

---

### **Day 2: Monday, 20th October - Tech & Innovation Deep Dive**

**Theme:** Immersion into Lagos's thriving tech ecosystem, "Yabacon Valley."

*   **9:00 AM - 1:00 PM (4 hours): Yaba Tech Hub Tour**
    *   **Activity:** Visit the epicenter of Lagos's tech scene. Start at the **Co-Creation Hub (CcHUB)**, Nigeria's first open living lab and pre-incubation space. Engage with the community and learn about the local startup landscape. Proceed to visit other key players in the area like **Andela** or **Impact Hub Lagos**.
    *   **Booking:** It's essential to reach out to these hubs via their official websites at least 2-3 weeks in advance to schedule a visit or a tour.

*   **1:00 PM - 2:30 PM (1.5 hours): Lunch in Yaba**
    *   **Dining Option:** **White House Hotel.** Don't let the name fool you; this is a legendary local canteen-style restaurant famous for its delicious and affordable Nigerian staples like Amala and various soups. It's an authentic experience where the tech community often lunches.

*   **2:30 PM - 5:00 PM (2.5 hours): Co-working & Networking**
    *   **Activity:** Head to a modern co-working space like **CapitalSquare** or **Workstation** in Victoria Island. This provides an excellent environment to catch up on work and network with other professionals in a different part of the city.
    *   **Booking:** Day passes can often be booked online a few days in advance.

*   **7:30 PM onwards: Tech Networking Dinner**
    *   **Dining Option:** **RSVP Lagos (Victoria Island).** A chic, upscale restaurant and bar with a sophisticated ambiance. It's a prime spot for meeting with contacts in a polished setting. The menu is contemporary American with a global twist.
    *   **Booking:** Reservations are highly recommended and can be made via their phone number or website.

---

### **Day 3: Tuesday, 21st October - Corporate, Finance & Eko Atlantic**

**Theme:** Engaging with the corporate heart of Lagos and exploring its ambitious future.

*   **9:30 AM - 12:30 PM (3 hours): Victoria Island Business District**
    *   **Activity:** Focus on scheduled meetings within the financial district of Victoria Island, home to the headquarters of major banks, multinational corporations, and the Nigerian Stock Exchange.

*   **12:30 PM - 2:00 PM (1.5 hours): Power Lunch**
    *   **Dining Option:** **Shiro Restaurant & Bar (Victoria Island).** A stunning oceanfront restaurant offering Pan-Asian cuisine. Its impressive architecture and ambiance make it an ideal location for a high-level business lunch.
    *   **Booking:** Reservations are essential, especially for a table with a view.

*   **2:30 PM - 4:30 PM (2 hours): Eko Atlantic City Tour**
    *   **Activity:** Arrange a tour of **Eko Atlantic City**, the monumental land reclamation project and emerging smart city. This is a must-see for anyone interested in urban development, real estate, and the future of African commerce.
    *   **Booking:** Tours must be arranged in advance through the Eko Atlantic official website or by contacting their sales office.

*   **7:00 PM onwards: Exclusive Networking Evening**
    *   **Activity:** An evening at a prestigious members-only club like **The Metropolitan Club** or the **Lagos Motor Boat Club**. Access typically requires an invitation from a member. Alternatively, **The George Hotel Ikoyi** offers a refined lounge atmosphere perfect for informal business discussions.

---

### **Day 4: Wednesday, 22nd October - Academia & Cultural Immersion**

**Theme:** Bridging academic collaboration with an appreciation for Nigeria's rich artistic heritage.

*   **10:00 AM - 1:00 PM (3 hours): Academic Collaboration**
    *   **Activity:** Visit the renowned **Lagos Business School (LBS), Pan-Atlantic University** for potential research and academic collaborations. The campus provides a serene environment for intellectual exchange. Alternatively, visit the **University of Lagos (UNILAG)** for engineering, science, or humanities partnerships.
    *   **Booking:** Formal appointments with specific faculty or departments must be arranged weeks or months prior.

*   **1:00 PM - 2:30 PM (1.5 hours): Lunch**
    *   **Dining Option:** **The LBS Cafeteria** for an informal meal on campus or **Yellow Chilli (Victoria Island)** for celebrated Nigerian cuisine in a more formal setting on your way to the next activity.

*   **3:00 PM - 5:00 PM (2 hours): Nigerian Art & Culture**
    *   **Activity:** Visit the **Nike Art Gallery**. This massive, four-story gallery is home to an extraordinary collection of contemporary and traditional Nigerian art. It’s an inspiring and visually stunning experience.

*   **7:00 PM onwards: Cultural Evening & Dinner**
    *   **Dining Option:** **Terra Kulture (Victoria Island).** An educational and recreational hub for Nigerian culture. Enjoy an authentic Nigerian dinner and, if your visit coincides with a show day (typically weekends, but check schedule), watch a spectacular stage play.
    *   **Booking:** Dinner reservations are recommended. Check their website for the theatre schedule and book tickets in advance.

---

### **Day 5: Thursday, 23rd October - Industry Summit & Trade Show**

**Theme:** Engaging with industry leaders at a premier conference venue.

*   **9:00 AM - 5:00 PM (Full Day): Industry Event**
    *   **Activity:** Attend a hypothetical (but plausible) **"West Africa Future of Work Summit"** or similar industry trade show at the **Eko Convention Centre**. This venue hosts major international and local events. This day is dedicated to attending keynotes, panel discussions, professional development seminars, and networking.
    *   **Note:** In reality, you would check the Eko Hotel or Landmark Centre events calendar for October 2025 and register for a relevant event.

*   **12:30 PM - 1:30 PM (1 hour): Conference Lunch**
    *   **Dining Option:** Lunch will likely be provided at the conference. Alternatively, the restaurants within the **Eko Hotel & Suites** offer numerous options, from casual cafes to fine dining.

*   **6:30 PM onwards: Post-Conference Cocktails & Dinner**
    *   **Dining Option:** **Salma's Authentic Lebanese Cuisine (Victoria Island).** A fantastic choice for a relaxed yet sophisticated dinner to unwind and discuss the day's events with new contacts.
    *   **Booking:** Recommended, especially for groups.

---

### **Day 6: Friday, 24th October - Nature, Reflection & Farewell Dinner**

**Theme:** Experiencing Lagos's unique natural environment and celebrating a successful trip.

*   **9:00 AM - 11:30 AM (2.5 hours): Lekki Conservation Centre**
    *   **Activity:** A refreshing morning exploring this urban nature reserve. Experience the famous **Canopy Walkway**, the longest in Africa, offering a breathtaking perspective of the forest. It's a great way to clear your head before a long flight home.
    *   **Tip:** Go early to avoid the heat and crowds. Wear comfortable shoes.

*   **12:00 PM - 1:30 PM (1.5 hours): Casual Lunch in Lekki**
    *   **Dining Option:** **Danfo Bistro & Dives (Lekki Phase 1).** A fun, modern eatery with creative takes on Nigerian street food and international dishes.

*   **2:00 PM - 5:00 PM (3 hours): Personal Time & Trip Wrap-up**
    *   **Activity:** Return to your accommodation. Use this time for final report writing, follow-up emails, or simply relaxing by the pool.

*   **8:00 PM onwards: Farewell Dinner**
    *   **Dining Option:** **NOK by Alara (Victoria Island).** Experience high-end contemporary pan-African cuisine in a stunning architectural setting. The outdoor garden is magical in the evening. This is the perfect venue for a memorable farewell dinner.
    *   **Booking:** Reservations are absolutely essential and should be made well in advance.

---

### **Day 7: Saturday, 25th October - Last-Minute Souvenirs & Departure**

**Theme:** Last-minute shopping and a smooth departure.

*   **10:00 AM - 1:00 PM (3 hours): Arts & Crafts Shopping**
    *   **Activity:** Visit the **Lekki Arts & Crafts Market (Jakande Market)**. This is the best place to buy authentic Nigerian souvenirs, from wood carvings and leather goods to paintings and textiles. Remember to bargain politely.

*   **1:00 PM - 2:30 PM (1.5 hours): Final Nigerian Meal**
    *   **Dining Option:** **Bukka Hut (Lekki Phase 1).** Enjoy one last, delicious Nigerian meal at this popular and reliable restaurant chain known for its wide variety of local dishes.

*   **2:30 PM - 7:30 PM (5 hours): Final Preparations & Check-out**
    *   **Activity:** Head back to your accommodation to pack, check out, and relax before your flight.

*   **7:30 PM: Depart for Airport**
    *   **Activity:** Transfer to Murtala Muhammed International Airport (LOS).
    *   **Tip:** For an 11:30 PM flight, leaving at 7:30 PM provides a safe buffer for Saturday evening traffic and airport formalities.

---

### **Prior Bookings & Essential Information**

*   **Airport Transfer:** Book a reliable car service like **Blacklane**, **Sixt**, or a hotel-provided car in advance for your arrival and departure.
*   **Tours & Meetings:**
    *   **Tech Hubs (CcHUB, etc.):** Email their partnerships/visiting departments at least 2-3 weeks in advance.
    *   **Eko Atlantic City:** Use the official contact form on their website to schedule a tour.
    *   **Academic Institutions (LBS, UNILAG):** Formal requests must be sent to the relevant departments months ahead of your visit.
*   **Restaurant Reservations:**
    *   **Essential (Book 1-2 weeks ahead):** NOK by Alara, Shiro, RSVP Lagos.
    *   **Recommended (Book 2-3 days ahead):** Terra Kulture, Salma's.
*   **Event Registration:** If you plan to attend a real conference, register online as soon as your travel is confirmed to benefit from early-bird pricing.
*   **Transportation:** Use ride-hailing apps like **Uber** or **Bolt** for getting around the city. They are generally safe and efficient, but always verify the car and driver details before starting your trip. Be prepared for significant traffic delays ("go-slows"), especially during peak hours.
