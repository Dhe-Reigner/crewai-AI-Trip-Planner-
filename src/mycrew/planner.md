### 🌍 **Essential Nairobi Travel Guide**

Follow these local tips for a safe, smooth, and culturally rich experience.

#### **💵 Currency & Money**
*   **Currency:** Kenyan Shilling (KES). Approx. **1 USD ≈ 130 KES**.
*   **Best Practice:** Exchange money at a licensed Forex Bureau at the airport or in a major mall for the best rates.
*   **Cards & Cash:** Credit cards (Visa/Mastercard) are widely accepted in hotels and upscale restaurants. Carry KES for tips and market shopping.

#### **☀️ Weather & Packing (Late October)**
*   **Climate:** This is the "short rains" season. Expect pleasant days (**72-79°F / 22-26°C**) and cool mornings/evenings (**54-59°F / 12-15°C**) with a chance of afternoon showers.
*   **Pack This:** Layered clothing (fleece/sweater), a light rain jacket, neutral-colored safari attire, sun hat, sunscreen, and comfortable closed-toe shoes.

#### **🛡️ Safety & Security**
*   **Transport:** Using a pre-booked private driver (as budgeted) is the safest way to travel. Uber/Bolt are also reliable for shorter trips.
*   **Valuables:** Avoid displaying expensive items. Use your hotel safe for passports and extra cash.
*   **Walking:** It's safe to walk in areas like Karen during the day. Avoid walking alone at night.

#### **🤝 Cultural Etiquette & Key Phrases**
*   **Greetings First:** Always start an interaction with a greeting. "Jambo!" (Hello) or "Habari?" (How are you?) is polite and appreciated.
*   **Photography:** Always ask for permission before taking photos of people.
*   **Bargaining:** It's expected and part of the fun at open-air markets. Start by offering half the asking price and negotiate with a smile.
*   **Tipping:** It is customary and highly appreciated for good service.
    *   **Restaurants:** 10% of the bill.
    *   **Driver/Guide:** $10-$15 USD per person, per day.

#### **🗣️ Useful Swahili Phrases**
*   **Jambo!** - Hello!
*   **Asante Sana** - Thank you very much.
*   **Karibu** - Welcome (You're welcome).
*   **Nzuri** - Good / Fine.
*   **Hakuna Matata** - No worries!
