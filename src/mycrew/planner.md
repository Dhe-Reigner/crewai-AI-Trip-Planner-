### 🌍 **Pre-Travel Checklist & Local Advisory**

#### **🩺 Health & Safety**
*   **Yellow Fever Vaccination:** This is **mandatory** for entry into Nigeria. Carry your vaccination certificate with you.
*   **Malaria:** Consult your doctor about anti-malarial medication. Use insect repellent containing DEET, especially in the evenings.
*   **Water:** Drink **only bottled water**. Avoid ice unless you are at a high-end hotel or restaurant.
*   **Security:** Be discreet with valuables. Avoid walking alone at night. Rely exclusively on your pre-booked driver or vetted ride-hailing apps.

#### **💵 Currency & Payments**
*   **Currency:** Nigerian Naira (₦, NGN). Estimate: $1 USD ≈ ₦1,500 NGN (check rates before travel).
*   **Exchanging Money:** Use licensed **Bureaux de Change (BDCs)** at the airport or your hotel for the best and safest rates. **Avoid street exchanges.**
*   **Payments:** Credit cards (Visa/MasterCard) are accepted at all major hotels and restaurants. Carry sufficient Naira for tips, markets, and smaller vendors.

#### **🤝 Cultural Etiquette**
*   **Greetings:** Always start with a polite greeting ("Good morning," "Good afternoon"). It is considered essential.
*   **Right Hand Rule:** Use your right hand to give or receive anything, including business cards and money.
*   **Respect for Elders:** Address new contacts with formal titles (Dr., Mrs., Mr.) until invited to use first names. A slight nod shows respect.

#### **🧳 What to Pack**
*   **Professional:** Lightweight suits, breathable dress shirts/blouses. A blazer for air-conditioned rooms.
*   **Casual:** Smart casual outfits for dinners.
*   **Essentials:** Compact umbrella, light waterproof jacket, sunglasses, high-SPF sunscreen, and power bank.
*   **Documents:** Passport, Nigerian Business Visa, Yellow Fever certificate, flight/hotel confirmations.
