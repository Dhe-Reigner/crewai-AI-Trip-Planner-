### 📝 Essential Kenya Travel Guide

#### 💵 Currency & Money Matters (Pesa)
*   **Currency:** Kenyan Shilling (KES). **$1 USD ≈ 130 KES** (check current rates before travel).
*   **Payments:** A mix of cash and cards is best. Use USD for tips and major tour payments (bring crisp, new bills from 2009 or later). Use KES (withdrawn from ATMs in Nairobi) for markets and smaller purchases. Credit cards are accepted at major hotels and restaurants.

#### 🌦️ Weather & Packing Guide (Hali ya Hewa)
*   **Season:** Late October is the "short rains" season. Expect warm, pleasant days (22-27°C / 72-81°F), cooler mornings/evenings, and short afternoon rain showers that turn the landscape green.
*   **Packing Essentials:** Pack layers! Bring lightweight shirts, a warm fleece/jacket, comfortable pants (neutral safari colors like khaki/green), a rain jacket, closed-toe walking shoes, a wide-brimmed hat, sunglasses, and high-SPF sunscreen.

#### ❤️‍🩹 Health & Safety (Usalama)
*   **Consult Your Doctor:** Visit a travel clinic for advice on recommended vaccinations and anti-malaria medication.
*   **Safari Safety:** Always listen to your guide, stay in the vehicle during game drives, never feed animals, and be escorted at your lodge after dark.
*   **City Safety:** In Nairobi, use reputable taxis (Uber/Bolt) instead of walking at night and keep valuables out of sight.

#### 🙏 Cultural Etiquette & Tipping (Utamaduni)
*   **Greetings:** A friendly **"Habari?"** (How are you?) will be warmly received. The reply is **"Nzuri"** (Good). **"Asante sana"** means "Thank you very much."
*   **Photography:** **ALWAYS ask for permission** before taking photos of people.
*   **Tipping:** Tipping is customary and greatly appreciated.
    *   **Safari Guide:** $10 - $15 USD per person, per day (give at the end).
    *   **Lodge/Camp Staff:** Use the communal tip box, $10 - $15 USD per person, per day.
    *   **Restaurants:** 10% for good service.
