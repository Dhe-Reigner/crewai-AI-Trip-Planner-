### **Your Accommodation Strategy 🏡**

To give you the best of both worlds, this plan is built around a **split-stay strategy**. You'll start in a tranquil, nature-focused area before moving to the city's cultural heart.

*   **Part 1 (6 Nights in Karen):** Stay in a **"Charming Garden Cottage" Airbnb**. This area is lush, secure, and places you minutes away from Nairobi National Park, the Giraffe Centre, and the Sheldrick Wildlife Trust.
*   **Part 2 (5 Nights in Westlands/Kilimani):** Move to an **"Art-Filled Urban Oasis" Airbnb**. This central location provides easy access to museums, art galleries, craft markets, and Nairobi's best restaurants.

This approach minimizes travel time to activities each day and allows you to fully immerse yourself in two distinct sides of Nairobi.

### **Getting Around Nairobi 🚗**

While you mentioned renting a car, for a first-time visit to Nairobi, **self-driving is not recommended** due to unfamiliar road conditions and traffic patterns. The best approach, which aligns with your preference and the budget, is:

*   **For Tours & Day Trips:** **Rent a car with a professional driver/guide.** This is the standard for safaris (Nairobi National Park) and longer excursions (Lake Naivasha, Nokras Hotel). It's safer, more relaxing, and your guide will provide invaluable local knowledge. This has been budgeted for.
*   **For City Travel:** Use ride-sharing apps like **Uber or Bolt**. They are widely available, affordable, and the safest way to travel within the city for restaurant visits, gallery hopping, and shopping.

### **Essential Pre-Travel Guide 🌍**

#### **🛂 Visa & Passport**
*   **Passport:** Must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your return date (i.e., until April 29, 2026).
*   **Visa:** You must apply for an **Electronic Travel Authorisation (eTA)** online before your trip. The cost is approximately $51.

#### **☀️ Weather & Packing (Late October)**
*   **Expect:** A mix of sunny mornings and short afternoon showers (the "short rains" season). The landscape will be green and beautiful.
*   **Temperatures:** Pleasant days around 24-27°C (75-81°F) and cool evenings/mornings around 13-15°C (55-59°F).
*   **Pack:** Layers are key! T-shirts, a warm fleece or light jacket, a waterproof jacket, and comfortable walking shoes. Neutral colors (khaki, brown) are best for safaris.

#### **💵 Currency & Money**
*   **Currency:** Kenyan Shilling (KES). 1 USD ≈ 130 KES (check rate closer to departure).
*   **Cards & Cash:** Credit cards are widely accepted in hotels and restaurants. Carry cash for markets, tips, and small purchases.
*   **ATMs:** Use ATMs inside banks or malls for the best security. Inform your bank of your travel plans.

#### **🤝 Cultural Etiquette & Basic Swahili**
*   **Greetings:** Always start a conversation with a friendly "Jambo!" (Hello) or "Habari?" (How are you?).
*   **Photography:** ALWAYS ask for permission before taking a photo of a person.
*   **Tipping:** Appreciated for good service. 10% in restaurants, and $10-15 per person, per day for your safari driver/guide.
*   **Helpful Phrases:**
    *   **Asante sana:** Thank you very much
    *   **Karibu:** Welcome / You're welcome
    *   **Tafadhali:** Please
    *   **Hakuna Matata:** No worries!

#### **🛡️ Safety & Awareness**
*   **Be Aware:** Like any big city, be mindful of your surroundings. Avoid displaying valuables openly.
*   **Transport:** Stick to Uber/Bolt or pre-booked taxis/drivers. Avoid walking alone at night.
*   **Valuables:** Use the safe in your accommodation for your passport and extra cash.
